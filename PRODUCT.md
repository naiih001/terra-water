# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Everyday consumers looking for quality spring water. They're shopping for themselves or their household — not luxury seekers, but people who care about what they drink and where it comes from. They're browsing on mobile during a commute or on desktop at home. The interface should feel approachable, clear, and trustworthy.

## Product Purpose

Terra is natural spring water bottled at the source. The site sells the water and the story behind it: ancient mountain springs, pure mineral content, sustainable sourcing. Success means a visitor leaves feeling they understand exactly what makes Terra different — and compelled to order.

## Brand Personality

Refined, minimal, intentional. The brand communicates through clarity and restraint, not decoration. Every element earns its place.

## Anti-references

- Overdesigned / trendy patterns: no heavy gradients, glassmorphism, wood textures, kraft paper, doodle leaves, or generic "natural" tropes.
- Corporate bottled-water branding (bright plastic colors, aggressive calls to action).
- Generic wellness aesthetics (pastels, crystals, soft-focus stock nature).

## Design Principles

1. **Refined restraint** — Nothing decorative that doesn't serve clarity or the brand story. If an element can be removed without losing meaning, remove it.
2. **Source transparency** — The interface reveals the origin and craft, mirroring the water's own purity. Show, don't embellish.
3. **Approachable minimalism** — Clean and clear enough for mass-market comfort, refined enough to signal quality. Warm without being cloying.
4. **Rhythm over repetition** — Vary spacing, pacing, and layout across sections. Each component earns its own shape rather than repeating a template.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA standard. Adequate color contrast, keyboard-navigable shop/cart flows, reduced-motion alternatives for all scroll-triggered animations.
